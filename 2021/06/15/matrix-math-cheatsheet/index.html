Matrix Calculus

Reference: [The Matrix Cookbook](matrixcookbook.pdf (uwaterloo.ca))

Useful Conclusion（keep updating）

eq(69)：$\mathbf{x, a}\in \mathbf{R}^p:$
$$
\dfrac{\partial \mathbf{x}^T\mathbf{a}}{\partial \mathbf{x}}=\dfrac{\partial \mathbf{a}^T\mathbf{x}}{\partial \mathbf{x}}=\mathbf{a}
$$
eq(81)：
$$
\dfrac{\partial \mathbf{x}^T\mathbf{B}\mathbf{x}}{\partial \mathbf{x}}=(\mathbf{B+B}^T)\mathbf{x}
$$
eq(61):
$$
\dfrac{\partial \mathbf{a}^T\mathbf{X^{-1}}\mathbf{b}}{\partial \mathbf{X}}=\mathbf{-X}^{-1}\mathbf{ab}^T\mathbf{X}^{-1}
$$
brief proof of eq(61):

Using the definition of the trace (denoted as $tr$) of a matrix. Given matrix A, B, we have
$$
tr(AB)=tr(BA)\\\
tr(ABC)=tr(BCA)=tr(CAB)\\\
\dfrac{\partial tr(AB)}{\partial A}=B^T
$$
Here $\mathbf{a}$ is $a \in \mathbb{R}^{p \times 1}, X \in \mathbb{R}^{p \times p}, b \in \mathbb{R}^{p \times 1}$, therefore we have $\mathbf{a}^T\mathbf{X^{-1}}\mathbf{b}$ is a scalar, thus its trace is the value of itself. i.e.,
$$
\mathbf{a}^T\mathbf{X^{-1}}\mathbf{b}=tr(\mathbf{a}^T\mathbf{X^{-1}}\mathbf{b})
$$
So we can write:
$$
\dfrac{\partial a^TX^{-1}b}{\partial X}=\dfrac{\partial tr(a^TX^{-1}b)}{\partial X}=\dfrac{\partial tr(ba^TX^{-1})}{\partial X}=\dfrac{\partial tr(X^{-1}ba^T)}{\partial X}=(ba^T)^T \dfrac{\partial X^{-1}}{\partial X}=-ab^TX^{-2}=-X^{-1}ab^TX^{-1}
$$
